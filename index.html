about-me.txt

Hi, I'm a 16-year-old from Pakistan. My journey started back in 2019. I wanted to start a gaming YouTube channel like every other kid back then (the name is way too embarrassing to share). I thought it would be effortless—just screen record, upload, and get rich. My first video got 70 views, 3 likes, 12 dislikes, and a wave of hate comments. That reality check made me realize there was a real craft to this.

Over the next few months, I dedicated myself to learning video editing. I didn't even have a PC, so I edited entirely on a phone using Kinemaster. The grind paid off; my videos started pulling 1k-2k views each, and I built an 11k subscriber community before eventually burning out and quitting. When COVID hit, I got my first low-end laptop. It could barely run Windows, but I managed to force DaVinci Resolve to work. I started picking up video editing commissions through Discord.

Being a 10-year-old freelancer meant most people didn't take me seriously, so payouts were tiny, and I floated in and out of the scene for about three years while picking up Blender on the side. The turning point came in 2024. A friend told me you could build your own games on Roblox and earn Robux. For a 14-year-old, that sounded like black magic. He introduced me to HiddenDevs, a developer matchmaking community on Discord.

Since I already had a solid foundation in Blender, mapping out Roblox environments and modeling 3D assets felt completely natural. My very first commission was a simple low-poly simulator map. It took me two days of work and made me 700 Robux post-tax. It wasn't my first time making money online, but it was the first time I felt true fulfillment—getting paid to build something I genuinely loved doing.

For the next year, I pulled day-and-night grinds on commissions, leveling up my technical skills every single day. Along the way, I built an incredible network of creators and developers. That network turned into action when Amin and I co-founded GoUp.gg, a full game development studio where I now direct internal dev pipelines, manage studio finances, and oversee our community moderation teams.
