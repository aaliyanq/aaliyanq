my-journey.txt

I'm a 16-year-old from Pakistan. My journey started back in 2019 when I wanted to become a gaming YouTuber like pretty much every other kid at the time (and no, I'm not sharing the channel name 😭). Back then, I genuinely thought it was as simple as recording your screen, uploading a video, and watching the money roll in. Then I uploaded my first video. It got around 70 views, 3 likes, 12 dislikes, and a bunch of hate comments. Safe to say it didn't go how I imagined. But honestly, that was probably the moment I realized content creation wasn't just pressing record and hoping for the best—it was an actual skill that took time to learn.

Over the next few months, I got really into learning video editing. I didn't even have a PC at the time, so everything was edited on my phone using KineMaster. Eventually, the effort started paying off. My videos were getting around 1–2k views each, and I built up a community of about 11,000 subscribers before I eventually stopped uploading. When COVID came around, I finally got my first laptop. It was pretty terrible and could barely run Windows, but somehow I managed to get DaVinci Resolve working on it. Around that time, I also started taking video editing commissions through Discord and making some money from the skills I'd picked up.

Being a 10-year-old freelancer wasn't exactly easy. Most people didn't take me seriously, so the payouts were usually pretty small. For the next few years, I kept drifting in and out of freelancing while also teaching myself Blender on the side. The real turning point came in 2024 when a friend told me you could make your own games on Roblox and actually earn Robux from them. To my 14-year-old brain, that sounded like some kind of black magic. He ended up introducing me to HiddenDevs, a Discord community where developers connect and work together.

Since I already had some experience with Blender, building Roblox maps and creating 3D assets felt pretty natural to me. My first commission was a simple low-poly simulator map. It took me about two days to finish, and I earned 700 Robux after tax. It wasn't the first money I'd made online, but it was the first time I felt genuinely proud of what I'd earned. Instead of editing someone else's content, I was getting paid to create something I actually enjoyed building.

For the next year, I spent countless hours grinding commissions and improving my skills with every project I took on. Along the way, I met a lot of talented developers and creators, and slowly built a network of people I genuinely enjoyed working with. Eventually, that led to something bigger. Amin and I teamed up to start GoUp.gg, a game development studio where I now help manage our development workflow, handle studio finances, and oversee our community moderation team.
